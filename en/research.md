My group's work sits at the intersection of Transformer-based NLP and Indonesian language
resources. We focus on problems where existing models underperform due to limited data,
linguistic mismatch, or biased dataset design — and we build practical systems that deploy
into educational and theological contexts.

## Ongoing projects

### Batak Toba Language Corpus &nbsp;<span class="tag">2025 — ongoing</span>

UPH Internal Grant · Principal Investigator. Building a multi-domain Batak Toba–Indonesian
parallel corpus across religious, cultural, educational, and contemporary text. End-to-end
pipeline covers document collection, OCR, native-speaker correction, and preprocessing.
The corpus is intended as a foundation for future low-resource language models and
evaluation benchmarks.

### Bias Analysis in Indonesian QA Datasets &nbsp;<span class="tag">2024 — completed</span>

UPH Internal Grant · Principal Investigator. Investigated bias and fairness issues in FACQA
using Transformer-based baselines and quantitative fairness metrics. Contributed
recommendations for dataset documentation and mitigation strategies in Indonesian NLP.

### Extractive Summarization for Mind Map Generation &nbsp;<span class="tag">2023–2024 — completed</span>

UPH Internal Grant · Principal Investigator. Prototype system combining Transformer-based
extractive summarization and clustering to generate structured mind maps from scientific
articles. Evaluated with human judges and integrated into teaching activities.
