Hands-on demos I've built for UPH Informatics open-house events and school visits. They're
designed to introduce high-school students to machine-learning concepts in under five
minutes, using their webcams as input.

All demos were prototyped in <a href="https://teachablemachine.withgoogle.com/">Teachable
Machine</a>, then packaged as lightweight web apps. They're used primarily as conversation
starters about computer vision, training data, and the limits of ML models.

## Why these matter

Science communication is part of the job of a program chair, and good demos lower the
barrier for high-school students to imagine themselves studying Informatics. If you've seen
these at an event and want the source code for your own classroom, send me an email.
